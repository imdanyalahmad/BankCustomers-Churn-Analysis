# Key Conclusion

The analysis reveals that customer churn is strongly influenced by financial status, customer segment, and engagement with banking services. Customers with low credit scores (around 350) and those within certain salary ranges show a higher likelihood of leaving the bank.

Interestingly, premium customers also exhibit a high churn rate, indicating that even high-value customers may not be fully satisfied with the services provided. Additionally, churn patterns vary across specific age groups and genders.

Another important insight is that customers with below-average credit card usage tend to have a higher churn rate, suggesting that lower engagement with banking services increases the probability of churn.

Overall, the findings highlight the importance of targeted retention strategies, improved customer engagement, and personalized financial services to reduce churn and improve customer loyalty.

# Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following strategies can help reduce customer churn:

## Target Low Credit Score Customers
Banks should offer financial guidance, flexible payment plans, or credit improvement programs to customers with low credit scores.

## Improve Engagement with Premium Customers
Since premium customers have a high churn rate, the bank should provide better loyalty programs, exclusive offers, and personalized services.

## Focus on High-Risk Demographics
Marketing strategies and retention campaigns should focus on the specific age groups and genders that show higher churn rates.

## Encourage Credit Card Usage
Offering cashback, reward points, or discounts could increase credit card usage and strengthen customer engagement.
